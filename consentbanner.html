Consent Management Platform (GDPR & EDSG)

Cross-Brand Cookie Consent Management at Scale

I led the implementation of a CMP across websites for different car brands with GDPR, revDSG, and Google Consent Mode V2.

Legal Context & Technical Compliance

To meet modern privacy laws, our solution supported opt-in, granular consent, preference storage, and consent changes. It dynamically blocked non-essential scripts and synced consent signals with Google and third parties.

CMP Customization and Branding

Each brand had a unique identity. I tailored banner layouts, fonts, colors, and languages per brand guidelines using CSS, HTML, and accessibility standards—ensuring both function and form were met.

Cookie Auditing and Categorization

I conducted cookie audits to classify and categorize all scripts. These were sorted into Essential, Analytics, Marketing, and Functional, ensuring that cookies fired only after user consent.

Handling Complex Use Case: Embedded iFrames

For 3rd-party iFrames on external domains, I used postMessage API to pass the user’s consent from parent to iFrame—ensuring cross-domain compliance even where we lacked direct control.

Integration with Google Consent Mode v2

I configured the CMP to send real-time consent signals via gtag() or dataLayer for categories like ad_user_data and ad_personalization, securing compliance while retaining ad capabilities.

Scalable and Maintainable Architecture

The architecture supported multiple domains, brands, and languages. All GTM/CMP setups were centrally managed, modular, and ready for future scalability with minimal duplication.

Outcome and Learnings

We passed internal audits and legal reviews. Brands had visual control, users’ preferences were respected, and advertising remained compliant. I deepened expertise in privacy strategy, iFrame messaging, and cross-functional delivery.
