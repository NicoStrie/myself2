Conversion Rate Optimization Project

CRO Project for a Confidential Global Brand

As part of a broader digital optimization initiative, I led a high-impact Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) project for a globally recognized brand (name withheld under NDA). The objective was to identify and validate actionable changes to the brand’s e-commerce user experience that could increase conversion rates and ultimately drive revenue growth.

Project Planning & Hypothesis Development

The engagement began with a comprehensive audit of the customer journey using both quantitative data (Google Analytics, heatmaps, and funnel drop-off rates) and qualitative inputs (session recordings, user feedback, and stakeholder insights). My analysis revealed several points of friction on the product detail and checkout pages, including unclear CTAs, redundant form fields, and distracting visual elements.

Based on these findings, I developed multiple hypotheses aimed at increasing user intent and reducing abandonment. Working closely with the product and marketing teams, I prioritized hypotheses using a data-backed ICE (Impact, Confidence, Ease) scoring framework. The highest-priority hypothesis formed the basis for our A/B test: simplifying the checkout form layout and enhancing trust signals near the purchase button would lead to higher conversion rates.

Coordination with Developers and Stakeholders

To implement the test, I collaborated closely with front-end developers, UX designers, and QA specialists. I facilitated cross-functional workshops to align everyone on the goals, success metrics, technical constraints, and expected timelines. I also ensured that data tracking mechanisms were implemented properly, including custom events and variant segmentation.

In parallel, I maintained clear and continuous communication with key stakeholders—providing regular updates, soliciting feedback, and documenting all assumptions, decisions, and test parameters. This level of transparency helped build alignment across marketing, e-commerce, and analytics teams.

A/B Testing Execution & Analysis

The A/B test was launched using a robust experimentation platform, targeting a statistically valid sample size over a two-week period. I closely monitored the test to ensure data integrity and managed interim check-ins to verify variant behavior and rule out implementation errors.

Upon conclusion, the data showed that the variant outperformed the control, with a 2% increase in purchase conversion rate. The result was statistically significant at the 95% confidence level. Secondary KPIs such as bounce rate and session duration also showed marginal improvements, indicating a more intuitive user flow.

Implementation & Post-Test Impact

Following the successful test outcome, I led the rollout of the winning variant as the new default experience. I coordinated with the deployment team to ensure a smooth transition and implemented a monitoring dashboard to track post-deployment metrics.

In the weeks that followed, the uplift in conversion was sustained, translating into measurable revenue gains. The initiative was recognized internally as a model example of data-driven decision-making and interdepartmental collaboration.
