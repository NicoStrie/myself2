AI-Based Scoring Model for Real-Time User Intent Detection

Adaptive Personalization Through Real-Time Intelligence

As part of a broader personalization strategy, I engineered a scoring engine to detect user intent dynamically. This system fuels an intelligent pop-in delivery mechanism—nudging users toward conversion at the exact moment of opportunity, without intruding on their experience.

Moving Beyond Passive Analytics

Traditional analytics platforms capture retrospective insights. Valuable, yes—but static. My goal was to shift from passive reporting to active decisioning—powering UX elements with real-time behavioral analysis and algorithmic inference. Built initially for XXXXX (name not disclosed), the model now serves as a core UX enhancement component across multiple brands and verticals.

Behavioral Signal Processing

The client-side engine parses dozens of live behavioral signals per session, such as:

  • Time-on-page and scroll velocity
  • Cursor movements, click hesitation, and exit intent
  • Navigation depth and funnel progression
  • Idle detection, tab switching, and return frequency
  • Campaign and referrer attribution
These signals are scored against a trained weight matrix to calculate an evolving composite intent score—allowing for in-session adaptation and precision targeting.

AI Model Architecture

On the back end, I trained a classification model and modeling techniques in Javascript. The dataset included labeled sessions (conversion vs. non-conversion), engagement metadata, and contextual session variables. Output is a discrete intent tier: low, medium, or high. The logic was then compiled into a lightweight JavaScript decision engine capable of sub-100ms client-side inference.

Pop-in Decision Framework

The scoring model governs the pop-in lifecycle: trigger timing, eligibility, and content variant selection. Medium-intent users receive helpful reminders or product comparisons. High-intent users may be shown personalized lead forms. Low-intent users remain uninterrupted—preserving cognitive flow and reducing modal fatigue.

Privacy-Conscious by Design

The engine operates entirely within GDPR and Swiss revDSG constraints:

  • Activates only after user consent via CMP
  • Processes anonymized, session-scoped behavioral data
  • No persistent identifiers or storage
  • Asynchronous execution with no UI performance penalty
The model’s outputs are also compatible with experimentation tools, enabling A/B testing and KPI benchmarking.

Impact Metrics

Results from early production rollouts include:

  • 28% reduction in unnecessary pop-in impressions
  • 17% lift in lead form conversions
  • Notable improvement in average session duration and satisfaction scores
These gains stem from relevance, timing, and the absence of generic interruptions.

Future Scope & Scalability

This scoring model is now a foundation for broader UX optimization efforts. Its use cases extend to:

  • Intent-aware CTA rendering
  • Real-time product recommendation logic
  • Dynamic retargeting activation
  • CRM enrichment and lead scoring
Modular by design, it’s retrainable and deployable across web, mobile, and embedded interfaces.

Closing Note

This project marks a high point in my work at the crossroads of AI and UX. It has sharpened my skills in real-time behavioral modeling, ethical tracking design, and lightweight client-side intelligence. More importantly, it redefines what digital personalization can look like when guided by user signals—not assumptions.
